3. Neural Cache Protocol (Data Privacy)

Z-Realism is designed around the principle of local data sovereignty. All inference workflows are executed within the user-controlled environment, and no generated content is transmitted to external services or third-party analytics platforms.

The internal Neural Cache mechanism operates strictly at the infrastructure level and exists solely to ensure the stability and continuity of long-running neural inference tasks.

Session Manifolds: The platform utilizes essential session tokens (cookies) to associate the client interface with active Neural Task IDs. This is an engineering requirement that allows the system to safely reconnect to high-latency CUDA workloads in the event of a page refresh or transient network interruption. No personal data, behavioral analytics, or identifying metadata is collected, stored, or transmitted.
